Conor Dowdall | Guru

Conor Dowdall is the founder and head of the Śūnyatā Foundation. The foundation, established in 2006, operates an ashram and yoga centre that carries out educational and spiritual activities.

Dowdall worked in the construction industry for many years, performing back-breaking labour and injuring himself many times, mostly from walking into walls or other bits of furniture and things. After being bitten by a pig, he had a series of spiritual experiences, which resulted in him leaving construction behind and beginning to teach yoga. He eventually developed his own style of yoga which he calls Half Yoga that focuses on building physical strength in typically under-developed parts of the body like the fingers and toes.

Conor has authored over thirty books on the topic, including the New York Times bestsellers Inner Peace: A Yogi's Guide to Inner Peace and Fingers and Toes: A Yogi's Guide to Fingers and Toes.
