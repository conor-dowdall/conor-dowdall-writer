Conor Dowdall | Writer

Conor was born in Dublin in 1978 on the same day that Golda Meir died. That day, the 8th of December, would prove instrumental in Conor's life, and all of the people whose lives he ruined. When pushed on the topic, Conor has repeatedly claimed he is not sorry about any of it. and has flatly refused to apologise on many occasions.

Developing a taste for beer and whiskey at the early age of seven, Conor was descibed in many interviews with his parents as a torturous nightmare. In one interview, his mother saidHave you ever seen The Omen? Conor was a lot like Damien in that.

In his best-selling memoir, Echoes of Prometheus: Dancing in the Twilight Garden, Dowdall recalls the complexities of navigating the stark contrasts between his poverty-stricken early childhood and his overtly-opulent, hyper-indulgent teenage years. It was a real mind-f*ck. Everything about it was designed to make me the psychopath I am today. If it wasn't for actress, dancer, producer, and singer, Goldie Hawn, I don't think I would have made it.

A millionaire several times over, and with a catalogue of hundreds of thousands of poems, short stories, and novels, this happening author is pumping out material at a fierce rate, lads. The New York Times described his work as ... the greatest sequences of words ever put into sentences by anyone, anywhere - period! The Guardian reported that Mr. Dowdall ... is clearly a genius of the highest order. When I finish something that he's written, I feel like a member of my family has died.
